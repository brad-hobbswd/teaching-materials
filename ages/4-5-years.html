Essential Questions

Encourage children to think, investigate, predict, communicate, and solve problems throughout each study.

How do we learn by asking questions?
Why do scientists observe and record what they discover?
How can we solve problems by working together?
What happens when we try something a different way?
How do books help us discover new ideas?
What patterns can we find all around us?

Family Connections

Children entering kindergarten benefit from everyday experiences that encourage independence, curiosity, responsibility, and confidence. Invite families to read together every day, count objects while shopping, identify letters on signs, write shopping lists together, play board games, explore nature, and encourage children to ask questions about the world around them.
