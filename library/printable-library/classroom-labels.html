Teacher Tips

Make Your Classroom Labels Last

A few simple steps can help your classroom labels stay neat, organized, and durable throughout the entire school year.

Print on Cardstock

Cardstock provides a thicker, more durable label that stands up to everyday classroom use.

Laminate for Durability

Laminating labels protects them from spills, fingerprints, fading, and everyday classroom wear.

Color Code Learning Centers

Give each learning center its own color to help children quickly recognize classroom areas.

Add Pictures or Icons

Pictures help young learners, emerging readers, and multilingual learners identify classroom materials.

Print Extra Copies

Keep replacement labels ready in case one becomes damaged or your classroom organization changes.

Store Extras

Keep extra labels organized in a binder or folder for quick access throughout the year.

Encourage Independence

Clearly labeled shelves and bins help children learn where materials belong and support independent cleanup.

Update Throughout the Year

Refresh labels to match seasonal themes, classroom units, or changing learning centers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to Common Questions

Find answers to the questions teachers ask most often about creating, customizing, printing, and using editable classroom labels.

Are these classroom labels really free?

Yes. Every editable label included in the Classroom Label Studio is available at no cost for personal classroom use.

Do I need to install any software?

No. Everything works directly in your web browser. Simply customize your label, preview your design, and print.

Can I use my own fonts?

The studio includes several classroom friendly fonts. Support for additional fonts may be added in future updates.

Can I print multiple labels on one page?

Yes. Choose from several page layouts including single, 2, 4, 6, 12, 20, or 30 labels per page.

Can I save my labels as a PDF?

Yes. After finishing your design, use the download option to create a printable PDF.

Can I edit labels after printing?

Absolutely. Simply reopen the Classroom Label Studio, make your changes, and print updated copies whenever you need them.

What paper should I use?

Cardstock works well for classroom signs, while adhesive label sheets are ideal for bins, baskets, and storage containers. Laminating labels helps them last throughout the school year.

May I share these resources with other teachers?

You're welcome to share a link to Little Explorers Learning Hub so others can create and download their own labels. Please do not redistribute the files themselves without permission.

Are the labels suitable for preschool and elementary classrooms?

Yes. They are designed for Early Head Start, Head Start, preschool, prekindergarten, kindergarten, and elementary classrooms.

Will more templates be added?

Yes. New themes, seasonal designs, icons, fonts, and classroom resources will continue to be added over time.
