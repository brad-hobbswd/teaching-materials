Week One

Getting to Know Trees

Children begin observing trees through outdoor exploration, discussion, books, art, and simple science experiences.

Start Monday Next Week →

Weekly Focus

Children observe trees and begin asking questions about what they notice.

Question of the Week

What makes a tree a tree?

Vocabulary

Tree Leaf Bark Roots Branch Trunk

Materials

Leaves Paper Crayons Glue Magnifying glasses Clipboards

Monday

Introducing the Tree Study

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Place baskets of leaves, sticks, pinecones, and tree cookies on the table. Invite children to touch, sort, compare, and describe what they notice.

Materials
  • Leaves
  • Small sticks
  • Pinecones
  • Tree cookies (optional)
  • Magnifying glasses
👋 Morning Meeting

Greeting

"Good morning, friends! Today we are beginning a brand new study about trees."

Question of the Day

Have you ever climbed a tree?

Discussion

  • Where do trees grow?
  • What do you already know about trees?
  • What would you like to learn?
📚 Read Aloud

Suggested Book

A Tree Is Nice by Janice May Udry

Discussion Questions

  • What did you notice about the trees?
  • Why are trees helpful?
  • What animals use trees?
🧩 Small Group

Leaf Investigation

Children compare collected leaves by color, size, shape, and texture.

Materials
  • Leaves
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Sorting trays
  • Paper

Teacher Prompts

  • Which leaf is biggest?
  • How are these alike?
  • How are they different?
  • Which one feels rough?
🎨 Interest Areas

🧱 Blocks

Build a forest using wooden blocks.

🎨 Art

Leaf rubbings using crayons.

📚 Library

Add books about trees and forests.

🔬 Discovery

Observe bark, sticks, and leaves with magnifying glasses.

🎭 Dramatic Play

Create a forest ranger station.

✏ Writing

Draw your favorite tree.

🌳 Outdoor Investigation

Take a tree walk around the playground or school. Encourage children to look closely at trunks, branches, bark, leaves, and roots.

Look For

  • Different sizes
  • Different colors
  • Bird nests
  • Fallen leaves
  • Acorns
🎵 Music & Movement

Pretend to be a tree.

  • Grow from a tiny seed.
  • Stretch into a tall tree.
  • Sway like branches.
  • Drop leaves slowly to the ground.
💬 Closing Reflection

Ask children:

  • What surprised you today?
  • What did you learn about trees?
  • What do you still wonder?
🏡 Family Connection

Invite families to take a short walk together and find one tree near their home. Children can draw the tree or describe it the next day.

Tuesday

Looking Closely at Trees

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Invite children to examine different leaves using magnifying glasses. Encourage them to notice lines, edges, colors, and shapes.

Materials
  • Collected leaves
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Sorting trays
  • Paper
👋 Morning Meeting

Question of the Day

Do all trees have the same leaves?

Discussion

  • Why are some leaves big?
  • Why are some small?
  • Do leaves always stay green?
🧩 Small Group

Leaf Sorting

Children sort leaves by color, size, shape, or texture and explain how they made their groups.

Materials
  • Collected leaves
  • Sorting mats
  • Paper
  • Crayons
🔬 Science Investigation

Compare fresh leaves with dry leaves.

  • Which bends?
  • Which breaks?
  • Which feels smoother?
🌳 Outdoor Exploration

Visit one tree every day this week. Encourage children to notice anything that looks different from yesterday.

🎨 Creative Art

Glue leaves onto paper to create a classroom nature collage.

🎵 Music & Movement

Pretend to be leaves blowing in the wind. Move slowly... Move quickly... Spin... Float... Land quietly.

💬 Reflection
  • What did you discover today?
  • Which leaf was your favorite?
  • How were the leaves different?
🏡 Family Connection

Invite families to collect three different leaves during an evening walk and bring them to school for Wednesday's investigation.

Wednesday

The Parts of a Tree

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Display leaves, sticks, bark, pinecones, and acorns on the table. Invite children to sort them by the part of the tree they came from.

Materials
  • Leaves
  • Small branches
  • Bark pieces
  • Pinecones
  • Acorns
👋 Morning Meeting

Question of the Day

What are the different parts of a tree?

Introduce Vocabulary

  • Roots
  • Trunk
  • Branches
  • Leaves
  • Bark
🧩 Small Group

Build a Tree

Children assemble a simple paper tree using pre-cut pieces while discussing each part.

Materials
  • Brown construction paper
  • Green paper
  • Glue sticks
  • Scissors
➕ Math Exploration

Count leaves collected from outside and compare which group has more or fewer.

🔬 Discovery Center

Feel different pieces of bark and describe how each one feels.

  • Smooth
  • Rough
  • Bumpy
  • Soft
🌳 Outdoor Investigation

Choose one tree outside. Point to each part together and encourage children to identify the trunk, branches, leaves, bark, and roots.

🎨 Creative Art

Paint with pinecones, leaves, or small branches instead of paintbrushes.

🎵 Music & Movement

Act out the life of a growing tree.

  • Curl into a tiny seed.
  • Grow roots.
  • Stretch up into a trunk.
  • Reach out with branches.
  • Wave leaves in the wind.
💬 Closing Reflection
  • Which part of the tree was new to you?
  • Why do trees need roots?
  • What helps trees grow?
🏡 Family Connection

Encourage families to visit a local park or neighborhood tree over the weekend. Invite children to notice birds, leaves, bark, and anything else that catches their attention, then share their discoveries when they return on Monday.

Thursday

How Trees Help Living Things

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Set out small toy animals, birds, and insects with sticks, leaves, and blocks. Invite children to create homes using natural materials.

Materials
  • Toy animals
  • Small birds
  • Leaves
  • Sticks
  • Wooden blocks
  • Pinecones
👋 Morning Meeting

Question of the Day

Who lives in trees?

Discussion

  • Do birds need trees?
  • Do squirrels need trees?
  • How do trees help us?
  • What happens if there are no trees?
📚 Read Aloud

Suggested Book

The Busy Tree by Jennifer Ward

Discussion Questions

  • Which animals visited the tree?
  • Why is the tree important?
  • Would you like to visit that tree?
🧩 Small Group

Build an Animal Home

Children use recycled materials to create a home for a bird, squirrel, or insect.

Materials
  • Cardboard
  • Leaves
  • Twigs
  • Glue
  • Paper
🔬 Science Investigation

Observe birds or insects outdoors and discuss how they use trees for shelter, food, or protection.

🎨 Interest Areas

🧱 Blocks

Build a forest for woodland animals.

🎭 Dramatic Play

Pretend to be forest animals searching for food.

📚 Library

Add books about forests and woodland animals.

🔬 Discovery

Compare feathers, leaves, sticks, and pinecones.

🎨 Art

Create birds using paper and natural materials.

➕ Math

Count birds, nests, or acorns in pictures.

🌳 Outdoor Investigation

Look for signs that animals use trees. Search for nests, holes, feathers, squirrel activity, or insects living on bark.

🎵 Music & Movement

Move like forest animals.

  • Fly like birds.
  • Hop like squirrels.
  • Crawl like insects.
  • Stretch like tall trees.
💬 Closing Reflection
  • Which animals need trees?
  • How do trees help birds?
  • How do trees help people?
🏡 Family Connection

Invite families to watch for birds, squirrels, or insects using trees during a walk and talk about what they observe together.

Friday

Celebrating What We Learned About Trees

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Invite children to revisit the natural materials collected throughout the week. Encourage them to create their own tree display or nature museum.

Materials
  • Leaves
  • Sticks
  • Pinecones
  • Acorns
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Nature journals
👋 Morning Meeting

Question of the Day

What is your favorite thing you learned about trees this week?

Discussion

  • What surprised you?
  • What was your favorite activity?
  • What would you still like to learn?
🎲 Tree Review Game

Show children collected nature items one at a time and invite them to name each object and explain how it is connected to a tree.

Materials
  • Leaves
  • Bark
  • Branches
  • Pinecones
  • Seeds
  • Acorns
🧩 Small Group

Create a Class Tree

Work together to create one large classroom tree. Each child adds a leaf with their name or a drawing of something they learned.

Materials
  • Bulletin board paper
  • Brown paper
  • Green paper leaves
  • Glue sticks
  • Markers
📚 Literacy

Invite each child to complete the sentence:

"I learned that trees..."

Write each response and read them aloud during closing circle.

🌳 Outdoor Celebration Walk

Visit your favorite tree one last time this week. Encourage children to notice anything new they did not observe on Monday.

🎵 Music & Movement

Play soft instrumental music while children pretend to be growing trees.

  • Grow from seeds.
  • Stretch toward the sun.
  • Sway in the breeze.
  • Drop colorful leaves.
  • Rest quietly for winter.
💬 Reflection Circle

Ask Children

  • What did you enjoy most this week?
  • What did you discover?
  • What would you like to explore next week?
📝 Teacher Documentation

Record children's questions, observations, new vocabulary, and interests to help guide Week 2 investigations.

🏡 Family Connection

Encourage families to visit a local park or neighborhood tree over the weekend. Invite children to notice birds, leaves, bark, and anything else that catches their attention, then share their discoveries when they return on Monday.
