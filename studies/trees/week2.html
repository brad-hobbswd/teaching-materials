Trees Study • Week 2

Parts of a Tree

Children explore how trees are built and discover the important jobs of roots, trunks, branches, and leaves through observation, play, science, and creative experiences.

Big Idea

Trees are living things with different parts that help them grow and survive.

Vocabulary

Roots, trunk, bark, branch, leaf, seed, tree, grow.

Science Focus

Children investigate how each part of a tree has a purpose.

Family Connection

Families explore trees around their homes and neighborhoods.

Monday: Discovering Tree Parts

Children investigate the different parts that make up a tree.

Circle Time & Discussion

Show photographs of different trees. Ask children what they notice. Introduce the words roots, trunk, branches, and leaves.

Science Investigation

Examine tree pieces such as bark, leaves, sticks, and seeds using magnifying glasses.

Materials

  • Tree photos
  • Natural tree items
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Science journals

Tuesday: Roots Beneath the Surface

Children explore how roots help trees stay strong and receive what they need to grow.

Arrival Invitation

Provide pictures of trees with visible roots. Invite children to look closely and discuss what they think roots do underground.

Morning Meeting

Discuss how roots anchor trees in the ground and help absorb water. Use movement by pretending to grow roots downward and branches upward.

Read Aloud Connection

Read a tree-themed story that shows how trees grow and change. Encourage children to make connections between the story and real trees they have seen.

Small Group Investigation

Children create pretend underground root systems using yarn, string, or natural materials. Discuss how roots spread and support trees.

STEM Challenge

Children design a tree structure that can stand tall using classroom building materials. Explore how roots provide support.

Art Experience

Children create root artwork by painting lines that show how roots grow beneath the soil.

Materials

  • Yarn or string
  • Tree photographs
  • Building materials
  • Brown and green art supplies
  • Paper and paint

Wednesday: Trunks and Bark

Children investigate how tree trunks support trees and protect them.

Arrival Invitation

Place pieces of bark, tree photographs, and wood samples in the discovery area for children to observe.

Morning Meeting

Discuss how the trunk helps carry water throughout the tree and how bark protects the tree like a covering.

Sensory Exploration

Children compare textures of different bark pieces using touch, sight, and descriptive words.

Science Investigation

Use magnifying glasses to observe bark patterns, lines, and textures. Encourage children to notice similarities and differences.

Math Connection

Children compare the sizes of tree trunks and sort natural materials by size, shape, and texture.

Writing Connection

Children draw a tree and label the parts they recognize or dictate observations about trees.

Interest Areas

Science Center

Tree samples, magnifiers, and observation tools.

Writing Center

Tree journals and vocabulary cards.

Art Center

Bark rubbings and tree texture artwork.

Thursday: Branches and Leaves

Children investigate how branches and leaves help trees grow, create energy, and connect with the world around them.

Arrival Invitation

Invite children to explore different types of leaves. Encourage them to compare colors, shapes, sizes, and textures.

Morning Meeting

Discuss how branches hold leaves and how leaves help trees make food. Talk about why leaves change throughout the year.

Read Aloud Connection

Read a story about trees, leaves, or nature. Encourage children to identify parts of trees they recognize.

Small Group Investigation

Children sort leaves by size, shape, color, and texture. Encourage children to describe their observations using new vocabulary.

Math Connection

Children count leaves, compare groups, and explore patterns found in nature.

Art Experience

Children create leaf prints using paint and collected leaves to explore patterns and textures.

Outdoor Exploration

Take children outside to observe branches, leaves, and different tree shapes. Encourage children to notice how trees look different from one another.

Materials

  • Leaves from different trees
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Sorting trays
  • Paint and paper
  • Nature journals

Friday: Creating Our Tree Models

Children combine their learning by creating models that show the important parts of a tree.

Arrival Invitation

Provide tree photos, natural materials, and building supplies for children to explore and plan their own tree creations.

Morning Meeting

Review the parts of a tree including roots, trunk, bark, branches, leaves, and seeds. Invite children to share what they learned during the week.

STEM Challenge

Children work together to design and build tree models using recycled materials, blocks, cardboard, and natural items.

Creative Project

Create a classroom tree mural where children add roots, trunks, branches, leaves, and other details they discovered during the study.

Music & Movement

Children move like trees by stretching tall like trunks, reaching like branches, and swaying like leaves in the wind.

Reflection Circle

Discuss what children learned about tree parts. Ask questions such as: “What part of the tree helps it stay in the ground?” and “What part grows on the branches?”

Family Connection

Invite families to look at trees together and talk about the different parts they notice. Encourage children to share their tree vocabulary at home.

Materials

  • Cardboard and recycled materials
  • Blocks and construction materials
  • Leaves and natural items
  • Glue and art supplies
  • Large paper for classroom mural
WEEK REFLECTION

What We Learned About Trees

Throughout this week, children explored the important parts of trees and discovered how each part helps trees grow and survive.

Science Discovery

Children identified roots, trunks, bark, branches, and leaves while learning how each part supports a tree.

Creative Expression

Children represented their learning through artwork, building, movement, and classroom tree creations.

Vocabulary Growth

Children practiced using new words to describe trees and their observations.

Family Connection

Invite families to take a nature walk together and notice the different parts of trees in their community.

Family Tree Observation

  • Find a tree near your home.
  • Look for roots, bark, branches, and leaves.
  • Talk about how trees help people, animals, and the environment.
  • Share discoveries with the classroom.
