🌳 WEEK TWO

The Parts of a Tree

Children investigate the different parts of trees including roots, trunks, bark, branches, and leaves while discovering how each part helps trees grow and survive.

Start Week → ← Previous Week
THIS WEEK WE EXPLORE

How Trees Are Built

Children learn that every part of a tree has an important purpose. Through investigation, play, and creativity, children discover how trees grow and stay healthy.

Big Idea

Trees are living things made of different parts that work together to help them grow.

Vocabulary

Roots, trunk, bark, branches, leaves, canopy, seeds, growth.

Science Focus

Children investigate the purpose of each tree part and how trees survive.

Family Connection

Families explore trees nearby and identify the parts they notice together.

DAY ONE

Monday

Discovering Tree Parts

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Place photographs of different trees, leaves, bark pieces, and natural materials in the discovery area. Invite children to observe what they notice.

💬 Morning Meeting

Introduce the parts of a tree. Discuss roots, trunk, bark, branches, and leaves. Ask children what they think each part does.

📚 Read Aloud Connection

Read a tree-themed book and encourage children to identify the different parts of trees shown in the story.

🔎 Small Group Investigation

Children examine tree materials using magnifying glasses. Encourage children to compare textures, shapes, and patterns.

Materials

  • Tree photographs
  • Leaves
  • Bark pieces
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Science journals
DAY TWO

Tuesday

Roots Beneath the Ground

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Invite children to observe pictures of trees with visible roots. Ask children what they think happens below the ground where we cannot see.

💬 Morning Meeting

Discuss how roots help trees stay standing and absorb water from the soil. Children use their bodies to pretend they are growing roots and reaching branches.

📚 Read Aloud Connection

Read a story about trees, roots, or nature. Encourage children to make connections between the story and trees they have seen outdoors.

🔎 Small Group Investigation

Children create underground root systems using yarn, string, or natural materials. Discuss how roots spread and support trees.

🧪 STEM Challenge

Children design a tree structure using blocks and building materials. Explore how a strong base helps a tree remain standing.

🎨 Creative Experience

Children create root artwork by painting lines that show how roots grow underneath the soil.

Materials

  • Yarn or string
  • Blocks
  • Building materials
  • Paint
  • Paper
DAY THREE

Wednesday

Trunks and Bark

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Place bark samples, tree photographs, and wood pieces in the discovery area. Encourage children to look closely and share observations.

💬 Morning Meeting

Talk about how the trunk supports the tree and moves water throughout the tree. Discuss how bark protects trees.

📚 Read Aloud Connection

Read a story about trees and invite children to identify the parts of trees they recognize.

🔎 Small Group Investigation

Children explore bark textures using magnifying glasses. Compare rough, smooth, thick, and thin textures.

🔬 Science Investigation

Children observe tree materials and discuss how trunks and bark help trees survive.

🔢 Math Connection

Children compare tree trunk sizes and sort natural materials by size, shape, and texture.

🏡 Interest Areas

Science Center

Magnifying glasses, bark samples, and tree observation tools.

Writing Center

Tree journals and vocabulary cards for drawing and writing.

Art Center

Bark rubbings and tree texture artwork.

DAY FOUR

Thursday

Branches and Leaves

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Invite children to explore different leaves and branches. Encourage children to compare their shapes, sizes, colors, and textures.

💬 Morning Meeting

Discuss how branches hold leaves and how leaves help trees make food. Talk about how leaves can change during different seasons.

📚 Read Aloud Connection

Read a story about trees, leaves, or nature. Encourage children to identify tree parts they have learned about.

🔎 Small Group Investigation

Children sort leaves by size, shape, color, and texture. Encourage children to describe what they observe.

🔢 Math Connection

Children count leaves, compare groups, create patterns, and explore similarities and differences found in nature.

🎨 Creative Experience

Children create leaf prints using paint and collected leaves to explore patterns, shapes, and textures.

Materials

  • Leaves
  • Small branches
  • Paint
  • Paper
  • Sorting trays
🌳 Outdoor Exploration

Take children outside to observe trees. Encourage them to identify branches, leaves, and differences between trees in the environment.

DAY FIVE

Friday

Creating Our Tree Models

🌞 Arrival Invitation

Provide natural materials, photographs, blocks, and recycled materials for children to plan and create their own tree models.

💬 Morning Meeting

Review the parts of a tree. Invite children to share what they discovered about roots, trunks, branches, and leaves.

🧪 STEM Challenge

Children work together to design and build a tree model that includes important tree parts. Encourage problem solving and teamwork.

🎨 Creative Experience

Children create a classroom tree mural showing roots underground, trunks, branches, leaves, and other discoveries from the week.

🎵 Music & Movement

Children move like trees by stretching tall like trunks, reaching like branches, and moving gently like leaves in the wind.

💭 Reflection Circle

Ask children what they learned about trees. Encourage questions such as: “What part of the tree holds it in the ground?” “What grows from the branches?”

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Connection

Invite families to take a walk together and identify tree parts in their neighborhood. Encourage children to share their new vocabulary at home.

WEEK REFLECTION

The Parts of a Tree

During this week, children explored how each part of a tree has an important role. Through observation, science, art, movement, and play, children discovered how trees grow and survive.

Science Discovery

Children identified roots, trunks, bark, branches, and leaves while learning how each part supports tree growth.

Language Development

Children expanded their vocabulary by describing tree parts, textures, shapes, and observations.

Creative Thinking

Children represented their learning through artwork, building, dramatic play, and classroom tree creations.

FAMILY CONNECTION

Explore Trees Together

Families can continue learning by observing trees in their own neighborhoods.

Family Tree Observation Walk

  • Find a tree near your home or community.
  • Look for roots, bark, branches, and leaves.
  • Talk about how each part helps the tree.
  • Share discoveries with the classroom.
