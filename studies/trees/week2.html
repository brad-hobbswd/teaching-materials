Trees Study • Week 2

Parts of a Tree

Children explore how trees are built and discover the important jobs of roots, trunks, branches, and leaves through observation, play, science, and creative experiences.

Big Idea

Trees are living things with different parts that help them grow and survive.

Vocabulary

Roots, trunk, bark, branch, leaf, seed, tree, grow.

Science Focus

Children investigate how each part of a tree has a purpose.

Family Connection

Families explore trees around their homes and neighborhoods.

Monday: Discovering Tree Parts

Children investigate the different parts that make up a tree.

Circle Time & Discussion

Show photographs of different trees. Ask children what they notice. Introduce the words roots, trunk, branches, and leaves.

Science Investigation

Examine tree pieces such as bark, leaves, sticks, and seeds using magnifying glasses.

Materials

  • Tree photos
  • Natural tree items
  • Magnifying glasses
  • Science journals

Tuesday: Roots and Growing

Children learn how roots help trees receive water and stay strong.

STEM Challenge

Create a model tree using classroom materials. Children design roots that help their tree stand tall.

Art Experience

Children create root paintings using branches, string, or yarn.

Wednesday: Trunks and Bark

Children explore how trunks support trees and protect them.

Sensory Exploration

Children compare tree bark textures using touch and observation.

Writing Connection

Children draw a tree and label the parts they recognize.

Thursday: Branches and Leaves

Children investigate how branches and leaves help trees grow.

Outdoor Exploration

Take children outside to observe branches, leaves, and different tree shapes.

Math Connection

Sort leaves by size, shape, and color.

Friday: Creating Our Tree Models

Children combine their learning into a final tree creation.

Creative Project

Children create a classroom tree mural showing roots, trunk, branches, and leaves.

Reflection

Discuss what children learned about how trees grow and why trees are important.

Learning Goals Achieved

Science

Children identify basic tree parts and their functions.

Language

Children use new vocabulary while describing trees.

Creativity

Children represent learning through art and construction.
