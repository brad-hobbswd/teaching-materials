Study Overview

The Trees Study encourages children to observe, question, compare, measure, create, and discover the important role trees play in our environment.

Four Week Investigation

Each week builds children's understanding through exploration, observation, discussion, and hands on learning.

Week 1

Getting to Know Trees

Children begin observing trees, identifying their parts, and sharing what they already know.

  • Tree walks
  • Observing bark
  • Leaf collecting
  • Nature journals
  • Tree vocabulary
Open Week →
Week 2

How Trees Grow

Children investigate roots, seeds, leaves, water, sunlight, and seasons.

  • Planting seeds
  • Growth observations
  • Measuring trees
  • Comparing leaves
  • Weather connections
Open Week →
Week 3

Trees and Living Things

Explore animals, insects, birds, nests, habitats, and food that trees provide.

  • Bird watching
  • Animal homes
  • Insect investigations
  • Pinecones
  • Nature art
Open Week →
Week 4

Why Trees Matter

Children discover how trees help people, animals, and our planet.

  • Community trees
  • Wood products
  • Protecting nature
  • Recycling paper
  • Celebration project
Open Week →

Family Engagement

Tree Walk at Home

Invite families to take a short walk together and look for different kinds of trees. Encourage children to collect one fallen leaf or draw their favorite tree to share with the class.
