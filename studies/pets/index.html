🐾 Pets Study

Children investigate the wonderful world of pets by learning how animals grow, communicate, play, and depend on people for love and care. Through meaningful experiences, children build empathy, responsibility, scientific thinking, and an appreciation for living things.

❤️ Responsibility

Study Overview

Why Study Pets?

Many children have pets at home or know someone who does. Pets provide opportunities to explore kindness, responsibility, communication, habitats, nutrition, life cycles, veterinary care, and the many ways animals enrich our lives. Children naturally ask questions, observe behaviors, compare animals, and develop compassion while learning about living things.

Hands On Learning

Four Week Investigation

Each week encourages children to ask questions, investigate living things, and develop compassion through meaningful experiences with pets.

🐾

Week One

What is a pet? Children identify different pets, compare animals, and discuss why families choose to have pets.

🥣

Week Two

Learn how pets are cared for by exploring food, water, exercise, shelter, grooming, and daily routines.

🩺

Week Three

Investigate veterinarians, animal hospitals, shelters, pet stores, and the people who help animals stay healthy.

🌎

Week Four

Compare pets from around the world while celebrating responsible pet ownership and kindness toward all animals.
