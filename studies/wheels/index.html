🚲 Wheels Study

Children explore the world of wheels through hands-on investigations involving motion, transportation, engineering, measurement, and problem solving. This study encourages curiosity as children discover how wheels help objects move and how engineers design systems that work.

SCIENCE • STEM • ENGINEERING

Learning Through Wheels & Motion

Wheels provide meaningful opportunities for children to investigate movement, cause and effect, design, and problem solving. Through building, testing, measuring, and exploring, children develop early engineering skills and scientific thinking.

Essential Questions

Questions that guide children's investigation and discovery throughout the Wheels Study.

🚲

How Do Wheels Help Us Move?

Children explore how wheels make transportation easier and help objects move from one place to another.

⚙️

What Makes Things Roll?

Children investigate movement, surfaces, size, shape, and how different objects roll.

🏗️

How Do Engineers Design?

Children design, build, test, and improve creations using early engineering skills.

LEARNING GOALS

What Children Will Learn

🔬 Science

Children investigate motion, force, speed, and how wheels help objects move.

🧮 Mathematics

Children compare sizes, measure distances, count wheels, and explore patterns.

🗣 Language

Children develop vocabulary while describing transportation, movement, and design.

🎨 Creativity

Children create vehicles, inventions, and artwork inspired by wheels.

HANDS-ON LEARNING

Classroom Investigations

Children explore wheels through science, engineering, math, art, and collaborative problem solving.

🚲 Rolling Experiments

Children test different objects to discover what rolls, what does not, and why.

⚙️ Build a Vehicle

Children use blocks, recycled materials, and wheels to design their own moving creations.

📏 Measuring Motion

Children measure how far objects travel and compare movement using different surfaces.

🔍 Engineering Challenges

Children solve problems by designing, testing, and improving wheel-based inventions.

INTEREST AREAS

Wheels Learning Centers

🏗️ Block Area

Build roads, ramps, bridges, and vehicles while exploring construction and design.

🔬 Science Area

Investigate wheels, axles, movement, and cause-and-effect relationships.

🎨 Art Area

Create wheel prints, vehicle designs, and transportation artwork.

📚 Literacy Area

Read transportation books and practice using new vocabulary.

🧮 Math Area

Count wheels, compare sizes, measure distances, and explore patterns.

🌎 Dramatic Play

Create transportation centers such as repair shops, garages, and airports.

HOME CONNECTION

Family Connections

Families can continue exploring wheels and movement through everyday experiences at home and in the community.

🚗 Explore Transportation

Take a walk through the neighborhood and look for cars, bicycles, wagons, strollers, and other objects that use wheels.

🔍 Ask Questions

Encourage children to wonder why some wheels are large, small, smooth, or designed differently.

🏗 Build Together

Create simple vehicles using recycled materials and test how they move.
