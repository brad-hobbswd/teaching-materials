Gardening Study

Children explore the amazing world of plants through hands-on gardening experiences. They investigate seeds, soil, weather, plant growth, and the important role gardens play in our environment and communities.

🌱 Plants & Growth 🔬 Science 🐝 Nature Exploration 💧 Life Cycles

Growing Curious Minds

Gardening gives children opportunities to observe, question, predict, and discover how living things grow. Through planting, caring for, and studying plants, children build scientific thinking skills while developing patience, responsibility, and appreciation for nature.

Essential Questions

🌱 What do plants need to grow?
🌱 How do seeds change over time?
☀️ Why do plants need sunlight and water?
🐞 What living things can we find in a garden?
🌎 How do gardens help people and animals?
💚 How can we care for plants and our environment?

Learning Areas

🔬 Science

Children investigate plant parts, seed germination, roots, stems, insects, pollinators, weather, and the needs of living things.

📚 Literacy

Children build garden vocabulary, create observation journals, read informational books, and share discoveries.

🔢 Math

Children count seeds, measure plant growth, compare sizes, sort natural materials, and identify patterns.

🎨 Creative Arts

Children create flower artwork, nature collages, garden signs, and plant-inspired creations.

Hands-On Gardening Activities

🌱

Seed Investigation

Children examine different seeds using magnifying glasses and discuss similarities, differences, shapes, and textures.

💧

What Plants Need

Children explore how sunlight, water, soil, and care affect plant growth through simple classroom investigations.

🐞

Garden Detectives

Children search for insects and explore how bugs, worms, and pollinators help gardens grow.

🌻

Plant a Classroom Garden

Children plant flowers, herbs, or vegetables and practice responsibility by caring for living things.

Classroom Learning Centers

🌱 Science Center

Seeds, soil, plant samples, magnifying tools, and observation materials for exploring growth and change.

📚 Library Center

Garden books, plant journals, nonfiction texts, and stories about nature and growing.

🎨 Art Center

Flower painting, nature collages, leaf prints, and creative garden projects.

🏡 Dramatic Play

Garden shop, farmer's market, flower shop, and pretend gardening experiences.

Recommended Books

📗

Family Connections

Families can continue gardening discoveries at home by planting seeds, caring for plants, visiting gardens, cooking with vegetables, and exploring nature together. Everyday experiences help children develop curiosity, responsibility, and appreciation for the world around them.
