🏙️ Buildings Study

Children investigate the buildings that make up their communities while discovering architecture, construction, engineering, neighborhoods, and the many ways buildings help people live, work, learn, and play.

🏗️ Engineering
🏠 Community
📐 STEM
🧱 Hands On Learning
Study Overview

Why Study Buildings?

Buildings are everywhere children go. Homes, schools, libraries, stores, hospitals, churches, fire stations, and restaurants all provide opportunities for investigation. As children explore buildings, they naturally develop mathematical thinking, scientific inquiry, engineering skills, language, creativity, and social understanding.

📅 Four Week Study
🧱 Engineering Design
📚 Literacy Rich
🔬 STEM Focused

Questions to Investigate

Encourage children to ask questions, make predictions, observe carefully, and solve problems throughout the study.

🏠

What Is A Building?

How are buildings alike and different? What makes a building useful?

🏗️

Who Builds Buildings?

Discover architects, engineers, construction workers, electricians, plumbers, and many other community helpers.

🧱

What Materials Are Used?

Investigate wood, brick, steel, concrete, glass, stone, and recycled materials.

🌎

Why Are Buildings Different?

Explore how buildings change depending on weather, culture, purpose, and location around the world.

Hands On Learning

Four Week Investigation

Each week builds upon children's questions while encouraging exploration, engineering, creativity, and problem solving.

🏠

Week One

What is a building? Children observe buildings around their homes, school, and community while identifying different types and purposes.

🏗️

Week Two

How are buildings made? Explore construction workers, tools, blueprints, building materials, and engineering.

🧱

Week Three

Design and build using blocks, recycled materials, cardboard, wood pieces, and loose parts while testing strength and stability.

🌎

Week Four

Compare homes and buildings around the world while designing an imaginative community together.

Teacher Tips

Follow Children's Ideas

Allow children's questions and discoveries to guide the direction of the investigation.

Collect Materials

Document Learning

Ask Open Questions
