👕 Clothes Study

Children investigate the clothing they wear every day while discovering fabrics, fasteners, weather, fashion, cultural clothing, careers, and the important role clothing plays in our lives. Through exploration they develop independence, observation skills, creativity, and scientific thinking.

👕 Self Help Skills
🧵 Fabrics
🌦️ Weather
🎨 Creativity
Study Overview

Why Study Clothes?

Children wear clothing every day, making it a familiar and meaningful topic for investigation. As they explore clothing, children compare fabrics, discover how clothes are made, investigate weather, practice dressing skills, learn about occupations, and appreciate clothing traditions from cultures around the world.

Hands On Learning

Four Week Investigation

Children investigate clothing through observation, comparison, dramatic play, design, and hands on exploration.

👕

Week One

What are clothes? Explore different types of clothing, identify clothing we wear every day, and compare colors, sizes, and purposes.

🌦️

Week Two

Why do we wear different clothes? Investigate clothing for weather, seasons, occupations, sports, and special occasions.

🧵

Week Three

How are clothes made? Explore fabrics, sewing, weaving, fasteners, clothing labels, and textile materials.

🌍

Week Four

Celebrate clothing from around the world while designing original outfits and sharing family traditions.

Learning Goals

Children strengthen every developmental domain while exploring clothing, fashion, and self care skills.

💬

Language

Describe clothing, compare fabrics, explain observations, and introduce rich descriptive vocabulary.

🔢

Mathematics

Sort clothing by size, color, texture, pattern, and purpose while measuring and counting clothing items.

🔬

Science

Investigate fabrics, absorbency, insulation, waterproof materials, and how clothing protects our bodies.

🧥

Self Help Skills

Practice buttoning, zipping, snapping, buckling, tying, folding, and caring for clothing independently.

🎨

Creativity

Social Emotional

Develop confidence, independence, respect for cultural differences, and appreciation for individual expression.
