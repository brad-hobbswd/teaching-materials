Preparing Confident Learners For Kindergarten

Pre Kindergarten is a year of tremendous growth. Children strengthen academic foundations while becoming confident problem solvers, communicators, collaborators, and independent learners. Every lesson encourages exploration, creativity, inquiry, and joyful discovery while preparing children for a successful transition to kindergarten.

Kindergarten Readiness Skills

Intentional learning experiences help children become confident, independent learners ready for kindergarten.

Literacy Readiness

🔢

Mathematics

Social Emotional Skills

Language & Literacy

Children strengthen communication, comprehension, phonological awareness, and emergent reading through purposeful daily experiences.

📖

Interactive Reading

Read quality literature while encouraging children to predict, infer, retell, and discuss stories using rich vocabulary.

🔤

Phonological Awareness

Practice rhyming, syllables, beginning sounds, ending sounds, and oral language games.

✏️

Writing Workshop

Encourage journals, sentence writing, labeling, book making, and authentic classroom writing opportunities.

🎭

Storytelling

Retell familiar stories, create original narratives, and perform dramatic retellings with puppets and props.

Mathematics

Mathematics becomes meaningful when children investigate numbers, patterns, measurement, geometry, and problem solving through play.

Numbers

Number Sense

Count beyond twenty, compare quantities, compose and decompose numbers, and solve everyday math problems.

Measurement

Measurement & Data

Measure length, weight, height, time, and capacity while creating graphs and recording observations.

Patterns

Patterns & Algebraic Thinking

Recognize, extend, create, and explain increasingly complex repeating patterns.

Geometry

Shapes & Spatial Thinking

Investigate two dimensional and three dimensional shapes while building and designing structures.
